Scripts

I wrote a handful of scripts for my strategic writing courses, three of which are displayed below. One is a public service anouncement for the radio that promotes healthy communication between parents and their kids. The affiliated website, talkaboutyourfeelings.org, is completely made up. The second script outlines a 30 second television promo for the final season of New Girl. At the time of writing the script I knew little about the final season, so again, the work is fictional. The third script is what could (but never will) be a television advertisement for backcountry.com, a client I chose to write for during one of my classes but otherwise have no affliation with.

  • PSA
  • TV Promo
  • TV ad
  • Client/Sponsor: talkaboutyourfeelings.org
  • Length: 30 seconds
  • Air Dates: October 29 – November 14

FATHER: "How was your day, son?"

SON: "Eh. It was okay."

FATHER: "Just okay?"

SON: "Yeah…"

SFX: A bell rings

ANNOUNCER: "Don’t let the conversation stop there. Find a way to talk to your kids about their feelings. Try empathizing with them."

SFX: The sound of a rewinding tape

FATHER: "Just okay?"

SON: "Yeah…"

FATHER: "It's ok to have a bad day, everybody has them. Last week at work, I came out of the bathroom with toilet paper stuck to my foot. My boss was visiting the office. He made a comment about it in front of everyone."

SFX: A boy’s laugher

SON: “Really?”

FATHER: “Yeah, I was embarrassed the rest of the day. Anyway, tell me more about your day, son?”

SON: “Okay, I just didn’t…”

SFX: Boy’s voice fades

ANNOUNCER: “Visit www.talkaboutyourfeelings.org for more suggestions. Talking to your kids about their feeling is vital to their mental health.”

E-blast Letter

I wrote this E-mail during the public relations portion of Writing for Strategic Communication, the same class I've mentioned above.


Dear Jordan,

How often do you take the beauty of nature for granted?

We asked ourselves the same question at Backcountry and realized we take it for granted way too often. As a result, Backcountry has decided to do more than just enjoy nature when we go camping, hiking or mountain biking. We decided to give back, to help maintain its beauty.

Last year, we gave a portion of what you spent during a limited-time sale to Easy Trees, an organization dedicated to connecting people to nature by building parks and planting trees in urban areas. This year, we are a planning a service project, and we want you to join us for it. By connecting with us on social media, you’ll be informed of the upcoming service opportunities.

Join us on social media to learn more:

  • Twitter - @backcountry
  • Instagram - @backcountry
  • Facebook - @Backcountry

The first-ever “Backcountry Gives Back” service project will be held near our headquarters in Park City. If you are a resident of Utah and want to volunteer with us, send us an email expressing your intent to volunteer along with your phone number. Our email is backcountrygivesback@backcountry.net. As a result, you’ll get a free t-shirt and a deeper appreciation for the beauty of nature. We promise!

In the meantime, keep stocking up on gear at backcountry.com and make time to get outside and do what you love.

Best,

Signature Jones

Head of PR, Backcountry, Inc.
